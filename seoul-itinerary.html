🏯 Gyeongbokgung Palace + Hanbok Rental
Start your trip at Seoul's grandest palace. Rent hanbok from shops just outside the gates — your daughter becomes a princess, and you both get free entry! The Guard Changing Ceremony runs at 10am, 1pm & 3pm at Gwanghwamun Gate — arrive 15 mins early for a great spot.

🍜 Lunch near the Palace
Head to streets around Gyeongbokgung for bibimbap or gimbap at a local spot. Kids love gimbap (Korean rice rolls) — cheap, filling and delicious.
₩7,000–12,000 per person

PM
🏛️ National Folk Museum of Korea
Located inside Gyeongbokgung's grounds. Free entry with a dedicated children's section — hands-on traditional games, costumes and interactive exhibits perfect for a 10-year-old. A great rainy-day backup too.
Free entryKid-friendly interactive exhibits

PM
🍢 Insadong Street Food & Crafts
A 10-min walk from the palace. Browse art galleries, traditional craft shops, and snack on hotteok (sweet pancakes), tteokbokki and tornado potatoes. Great for affordable souvenirs too.
Snacks ₩2,000–5,000 eachGreat for souvenirs

🏘️ Bukchon Hanok Village (Morning)
Go early — the narrow lanes of traditional hanok houses are magical and quiet before crowds arrive. Stunning viewpoints overlooking modern Seoul. Visit within the 10am–5pm tourist window and keep voices low (residents live here).
Free to exploreAvoid Sundays (quiet zone)~15 min walk from palace

🍵 Hanok Café Break
Stop at one of the beautiful traditional cafés in or near Bukchon for sikhye (sweet rice punch) or bingsu (shaved ice). Your daughter will love it.
₩5,000–10,000 per drink/dessert
1:30
PM
🌿 Changdeokgung Palace + Secret Garden
A UNESCO World Heritage Site and more serene than Gyeongbokgung. The highlight is the Huwon Secret Garden — a stunning 78-acre royal garden with ponds and forested paths. Book timed entry tickets in advance or arrive early.
Free with hanbokOtherwise ~₩3,000 adult / ₩1,500 childClosed Mondays — open today ✓
5:00
PM
🍲 Dinner: Tosokchon Samgyetang
Near the palace, this famous restaurant serves ginseng chicken soup (samgyetang) — a warming Korean classic. A genuine cultural food experience at a very reasonable price.
~₩17,000 per bowlIconic Seoul experience

🎢 Lotte World Theme Park
The world's largest indoor theme park — a full day of thrills for you both! Rides, parades, and the iconic Lotte World Adventure Kingdom. Friday is a weekday so queues will be 30–50% shorter than weekends — you'll spend more time on rides and less time waiting. Take subway Line 2 to Jamsil Station.
One-day pass ~₩62,000/person · Book online for discount✅ Weekday = short queues!Opens 9:30 AM — arrive early

🍽️ Lunch: Lotte World Food Court
Refuel inside the park. The food court offers Korean staples like tteokbokki, kimbap, ramen, and Korean fried chicken at reasonable park prices. Grab a banana milk to keep the energy going!
~₩10,000–15,000 per person inside park
2:00
PM
🎠 Afternoon Rides & Magic Island
After lunch, hit the outdoor Magic Island section of Lotte World — a floating amusement park on Seokchon Lake. The views of the lake with spring cherry blossoms are stunning. Don't miss the Gyro Swing or the Giant Loop if she's feeling brave!
Outdoor section — check weatherLake views with cherry blossoms 🌸

🗼 N Seoul Tower at Sunset
Head from Jamsil to Namsan for golden hour. Take the cable car up and watch the city turn amber from the tower observation deck. Add a love lock as a special father-daughter memory! The tower lights up beautifully after dark.
Cable car ~₩9,500 return · Observatory ~₩16,000/person🔒 Father-daughter love lock!Book sunset slot — fills up fast

🍜 Dinner: Myeongdong Street Food
Wind down the big day with an easy, fun street food dinner in nearby Myeongdong. Tornado potatoes, Korean corn dogs, tteokbokki, and mango bingsu for dessert. A perfect, low-effort end to the most adventurous day!
Dinner ~₩10,000–15,000 per person5 min walk from Myeongdong Station

🌸 Yeouido Hangang Park – Blossom Picnic
April 16–21 is right in peak blossom season! Over 1,800 cherry trees line the Han River at Yeouido. Grab snacks from a convenience store and have a picnic under the pink canopy — pure magic. Saturday means a lively festival atmosphere with locals out in full force enjoying the blooms.
Completely freePicnic snacks ₩10,000–15,000Subway Line 5 → Yeouinaru Station
1:00
PM
🚲 Bike the River Paths
Rent bikes and cycle the cherry blossom-lined riverbank paths — flat, easy terrain perfect for a 10-year-old. Utterly gorgeous during blossom season. The whole family atmosphere on a Saturday makes it even more festive!
~₩3,000–5,000 per hour per bikeFlat & kid-safe paths
4:30
PM
⛴️ Han River Evening Cruise
End the blossom day with a magical sunset cruise on the Han River. Glide past the Banpo Rainbow Bridge, spot city lights reflecting on the water, and feed seagulls from the deck. The E-Land cruise departs from Yeouido pier. Book well ahead — cherry blossom Saturday is the busiest night of the year!
~₩18,000/person · Book online in advanceDeparts Yeouido Hangang Park pier💡 Book ASAP — sells out on Saturdays!
💰 Est. daily spend (2 people): ₩65,000–90,000 (~AUD $70–95)

🛍️ Myeongdong – Last Souvenirs
Stroll Seoul's famous shopping street for last-minute gifts, K-beauty products and street snacks. Good for Korean snack packs to bring home. Compare prices before buying — some stalls are more expensive than markets.
K-beauty face masks from ₩1,000 each
6:00
PM
🍜 Farewell Dinner: Myeongdong Kyoja
End the trip with handmade knife-cut noodles and dumplings at this legendary Myeongdong institution. The kalguksu noodles are incredible and very budget-friendly — the perfect final meal.
~₩9,000–12,000 per bowlMay be cash only
💰 Est. daily spend (2 people): ₩40,000–60,000 (~AUD $43–65)
⚠️
Good to know: In Insadong and Hongdae, be wary of strangers inviting you to a "traditional ceremony" — this can end in pressure for large "offering fees." Also, some Myeongdong stalls charge more than local markets like Gwangjang. Stick to prices clearly displayed.

🗺️ Practical Info

🚇
Getting Around
T-money card for subway & bus (~₩2,500/ride). Taxi via Kakao T app.
🏯
Palace Station
Gyeongbokgung Station (Line 3, Exit 5) — 3 min walk to palace.
🌡️
April Weather
15–20°C days, cool mornings. Light jacket + layers. Light rain jacket handy.
💳
Money Tips
Cards widely accepted. Carry ~₩50,000 cash for markets & small shops.
📱
Useful Apps
Naver Maps, Kakao T (taxi), Korail (trains), Papago (translation).
🌸
Cherry Blossoms
Peak in Seoul is early–mid April. April 16–21 catches late blooms and falling petals.
